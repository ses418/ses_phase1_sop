Standard Operating Procedure

Module 1: Lead Management

Module Overview:

  1. Purpose & Scope:
    • The Lead Management module is your central hub for managing and analyzing all incoming business leads and project opportunities.
    • This system helps you track, categorize, and prioritize leads based on validation scores, project types, and geographical locations.
    • Access comprehensive information about projects, clients, and associated leads all in one unified interface.
    • Monitor lead progression from initial discovery through to conversion and active engagement.
  2. Key Features & Navigation:
    • Search Functionality: Use the search bar to quickly find projects by title, client name, or lead information.
    • Filter Options: Filter by Client/Lead, segment, and subsegment to narrow down your view and focus on specific categories.
    • Project Tabs: Navigate between "New Projects", "Viewed Projects", and "Conversation" tabs to organize your workflow.
    • Project Counter: Real-time count of projects in each category (e.g., 179 New Projects) helps prioritize your workload.
    • Data Table: Organized columns display S.NO, PROJECT TITLE, TYPE (PP/RD/GP/CP), LOCATION, VALIDATION SCORE, and LEADS count.
    • Type Legend: PP = Potential Project, RD = Research & Development, GP = Government Policies, CP = Completed Project.
  3. Understanding Project Types:
    • PP (Potential Project): Projects in planning or proposal stage requiring active pursuit and engagement.
    • RD (Research & Development): Projects focused on R&D activities, innovation, and technical development opportunities.
    • GP (Government Policies): Projects tracked via relevant government policy sources.
    • CP (Completed Project): Projects marked complete with verified outcomes.
    • The TYPE column helps you quickly identify the nature of each project for better categorization and follow-up strategies.
  4. Validation Score System:
    • Validation score reflects the count of verified source links for a project.
    • Example: score is 4 ‚Äî the project has verified evidence from 4 different sources; links are listed in news_mentioned_source.
    • Validation score does not relate to lead count; it is source verification only.
  5. Interactive Project Selection:
    • Click on any row in the project table to view comprehensive details about that specific project.
    • Accessing a project row reveals three key information sections: Project Details, Client Information, and Lead Data.
    • This interactive approach ensures you can quickly drill down into specifics without navigating away from the main dashboard.
    • All related information is presented in an organized, easy-to-read format for efficient decision-making.
  6. Getting Started:
    • Step 1: Start with the "New Projects" tab to see all recently added leads requiring initial review.
    • Step 2: Use the search and filter tools to focus on high-priority projects based on location, type, or validation score.
    • Step 3: Click on project rows to review detailed information and assess opportunity fit.
    • Step 4: Mark projects as viewed, initiate conversations, or generate cold emails based on your assessment.
    • Step 5: Track project progression through the tabs as you move leads through your sales pipeline.

User Perspective Guide - Complete Lead Management Workflow

Goal: Capture, qualify, assign, nurture, and convert leads efficiently.

Audience: SDRs, sales reps, and managers using the Lead Management page.

Quick Start

  • Add leads: import CSV, connect integrations, or capture via forms.
  • Qualify: verify contact info, apply lead score, add key notes.
  • Assign: route to the right owner by territory, product, or round-robin.
  • Engage: start a cadence (emails/calls), set tasks, and log outcomes.
  • Advance: update stage, schedule next steps, and move qualified leads to opportunities.

Prerequisites

  • Lead fields: name, email/phone, company, source, status/stage.
  • Routing rules: territories, industry, or round-robin settings.
  • Cadences: approved email templates, call scripts, and touch schedules.
  • Compliance: consent tracking for email/SMS; suppression lists enabled.

Capture Leads

  • Web forms: embed on site; map fields; enable double opt-in if required.
  • Imports: upload CSV/XLSX; dedupe by email/phone; preview mapping.
  • Integrations: sync from ads, events, chat, and landing pages.
  • Manual entry: quick add with inline validation and mandatory fields.

Qualify & Enrich

  • Profile checks: validate email format and phone; add role and industry.
  • Lead scoring: define points for fit (firmographics) and intent (activity).
  • Enrichment: pull company size, website, and tech stack if available.
  • Notes: add context from first touch; log pain points and timeline.

Organize & Assign

  • Queues: new, unworked, hot, and nurture queues by rules.
  • Ownership: auto-assign; allow manual reassignment with audit trail.
  • Priority: flag high-score leads; set due dates and SLA targets.
  • Tags: label by campaign, product interest, or event source.

Engage & Follow Up

  • Cadences: multi-touch sequences across email/call/LinkedIn.
  • Tasks: create call tasks; schedule reminders and next steps.
  • Templates: use approved messages with personalization tokens.
  • Logging: record outcomes (connected/not, interest, objections) and next action.

Pipeline & Status

  • Stages: New, Contacted, Qualified, Disqualified, Converted.
  • Disposition: reasons for disqualification; feed back to marketing.
  • SLA: time-to-first-touch and follow-up intervals; highlight breaches.
  • Conversion: create opportunity or proposal; link records for continuity.

Reporting & Analytics

  • Funnel: leads by source, contact rate, qualification rate, conversion rate.
  • Activity: touches per lead, response rates, and cadence performance.
  • Velocity: time from capture to first touch and to qualification.
  • Quality: duplicate rate, invalid contact data, and disqualification reasons.

Automation

  • Routing: auto-assign by territory or round-robin; reassign on inactivity.
  • Scoring updates: adjust scores on opens, clicks, replies, or visits.
  • Triggers: start nurture when unresponsive; escalate hot leads to priority.
  • Sync: push qualified leads to CRM; update suppression on opt-out.

Permissions & Safety

  • Access: role-based view/edit/export; restrict bulk actions.
  • PII: mask sensitive fields in lists; reveal only for authorized users.
  • Consent: honor opt-out; include unsubscribe in emails; log proof of consent.
  • Audits: track ownership changes, edits, and activity logs.

Troubleshooting

  • Duplicates: refine dedupe keys; merge records keeping recent activity.
  • Low contact rate: verify data quality; improve subject lines and timing.
  • Routing errors: review rules and territory mappings; fix owner assignments.
  • Cadence underperformance: test variants; shorten steps; adjust spacing.
  • SLA issues: set alerts; rebalance workloads; add backup owners.

Best Practices

  • Clear definitions: agree on lead fit criteria and stage transitions.
  • Single owner: one accountable rep per lead; watchers for stakeholders.
  • Short cycles: quick first touch; consistent follow-ups; stop when clearly unqualified.
  • Feedback loop: share disqualify reasons and top objections with marketing.
  • Continuous improvement: review funnel metrics weekly; iterate cadences and routing.

User Perspective Guide - Complete Cold Mail Workflow

Goal: Reach new prospects, start conversations, and book meetings with compliant cold email.

Audience: SDRs, founders, and sales teams running one-to-one outreach at scale.

Quick Start

  • Prepare inboxes: authenticate domains (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) and warm senders.
  • Build target list: define ICP, verify emails, and de-duplicate contacts.
  • Write sequence: 3‚Äì6 short touches across email and optional LinkedIn/call.
  • Personalize: add relevant hooks from prospect/company context.
  • Send safely: throttle volume, randomize timings, and monitor health.
  • Track outcomes: opens, replies, positives/negatives, and meetings booked.

Prerequisites

  • Authenticated domains: SPF, DKIM, DMARC aligned; separate subdomain for outreach.
  • Warmed inboxes: gradual daily send ramp; diverse content; real replies.
  • Consent posture: lawful basis for outreach per region; suppression lists enabled.
  • Data quality: validated emails, correct names/titles, and firmographic fit.

Build Target List

  • ICP filters: industry, company size, geography, seniority, relevant tech.
  • Sources: events, referrals, directories, intent data; avoid scraped low-quality lists.
  • Verification: check email deliverability; flag risky addresses (role accounts, catch-alls).
  • Hygiene: remove duplicates; enforce frequency caps and quiet hours.

Compose Emails

  • Subject lines: short, specific, non-spammy; avoid clickbait and ALL CAPS.
  • First line: personalized insight or trigger (recent post, hiring, tech change).
  • Value proposition: one clear benefit tied to their context.
  • CTA: simple ask (15-min intro, quick reply) with flexible timing options.
  • Format: plain text or light HTML; 60‚Äì120 words; no heavy images or links.
  • Signature: name, title, company, contact, and compliance footer.

Personalization

  • Tokens: {{first_name}}, {{company}}, {{role}}, with safe fallbacks.
  • Micro-hooks: recent news, hiring, stack, metrics, or shared connections.
  • Segmented templates: align messaging by industry and seniority.
  • Safety checks: validate tokens exist; skip send if personalization missing.

Deliverability

  • Ramp schedule: start 10‚Äì20/day per inbox; increase by 5‚Äì10/day weekly.
  • Throttling: limit hourly sends; add random delays and send windows.
  • Content checks: avoid spam trigger words; maintain text-to-link ratio.
  • List health: hard bounces auto-suppress; re-verify high-risk domains.
  • Reputation: spread sends across multiple warmed inboxes; rotate if needed.

Sending & Cadence

  • Sequence length: 3‚Äì6 steps over 10‚Äì21 days; vary angles and mediums.
  • Timing: business hours of prospect's timezone; avoid Mondays 9am/Fridays late.
  • Branching: stop on reply; pause on OOO; shift to nurture if no fit.
  • A/B tests: subject lines, CTA phrasing, and opener variants.

Tracking & Reply Handling

  • Signals: opens, clicks (sparingly), and replies categorized by intent.
  • Dispositions: positive, neutral, objection, not a fit, unsubscribe.
  • Next steps: schedule meeting, send case study, or hand off to AE.
  • Notes: log context and objections for future messaging improvements.

Compliance & Safety

  • Regional rules: CAN-SPAM/CASL/GDPR; include company address and opt-out.
  • Unsubscribe: honor immediately; add to suppression list; confirm removal.
  • Data privacy: minimize PII; secure storage; restrict exports.
  • Governance: approved templates; review for sensitive industries or claims.

Troubleshooting

  • High bounce rate: stop sends; re-verify list; check DNS authentication and send limits.
  • Low reply rate: improve ICP filters, first line relevance, and CTA clarity.
  • Spam folder: reduce links/images, avoid promotional tone, and slow ramp.
  • Blocks/rate limits: lower volume per inbox; distribute across accounts; extend delays.
  • Token errors: add fallbacks; implement pre-send validation; quarantine bad records.

Best Practices

  • Relevance over volume: tight targeting beats broad blasting.
  • Single ask: one clear CTA; remove friction by offering times or a lightweight reply.
  • Keep it human: natural language, no marketing fluff, and genuine curiosity.
  • Systematic follow-up: consistent cadence; stop on clear no; respect boundaries.
  • Continuous learning: review reply reasons weekly; refine templates and ICP.

Module 3: Newsletter

How to Operate:

  1. You can create a single newsletter for individual campaigns.
  2. You can also create multiple campaigns for newsletter distribution.
  3. Use the top header to switch between single newsletter and multiple campaign listing views.
  4. In the listing view, you can see the campaign name for each newsletter or campaign.
  5. Before the scheduled date, you need to preview and approve the newsletter.
  6. The review process is managed through the task management system.
  7. You can customize the newsletter template according to your requirements.
  8. You can also customize the email groups for sending the newsletter to specific recipients.
  9. Navigate through the listings to manage your newsletters and campaigns effectively.

How to Operate:

  1. Select the sections you want to include in your newsletter template.
  2. Upload or drag the sections to the canvas area to build your newsletter layout.
  3. Arrange the sections in your desired order by dragging and dropping them on the canvas.
  4. Customize each section by editing the content, images, and styling options.
  5. Preview your newsletter template to see how it will appear to recipients.
  6. Once you are satisfied with the design, click the "Save" button to save your newsletter template.
  7. The saved template can be reused for future newsletter campaigns.
  8. You can edit or update the template at any time before sending the newsletter.

How to Operate:

  1. View all the created email groups in the email groups section.
  2. Each group displays the group name and the number of contacts within that group.
  3. Select the checkbox next to the group(s) you want to include in your newsletter distribution.
  4. The respective emails from the selected group(s) will be grouped together for the newsletter campaign.
  5. You can select multiple groups by checking multiple checkboxes to combine different email lists.
  6. Review your selected groups to ensure you are sending to the correct audience.
  7. Proceed with scheduling or sending your newsletter to the selected email groups.

How to Operate:

  1. When you are ready to send the newsletter, you will see two options: "Generate Now" and "Schedule Generate".
  2. Click "Generate Now" to generate the newsletter content immediately; sending is not triggered.
  3. After "Generate Now", the campaign content is generated now but emails are sent only at the scheduled publish date.
  4. Click "Schedule Generate" to set a future publish date and time; content will be generated only within the 10 days before that date.
  5. With "Schedule Generate", content creation begins 10 days prior to the scheduled publish date.
  6. Set the desired start date and time for your scheduled newsletter campaign.
  7. The system will send the newsletter at the scheduled time upon approval.

How to Operate:

  1. Access the newsletter template customization section to personalize your newsletter design.
  2. Customize the newsletter template by editing colors, fonts, layout, and content sections.
  3. Add or remove sections from the template to match your newsletter requirements.
  4. Customize the email groups for sending the newsletter to specific target audiences.
  5. Select and configure the appropriate email groups that will receive the newsletter.
  6. Before the scheduled date, you need to preview the newsletter to ensure everything looks correct.
  7. Review the newsletter content, design, and recipient groups carefully.
  8. Approve the newsletter once you are satisfied with the preview.
  9. The review and approval process is managed through the task management system.
  10. Track the status of your newsletter approval through the task management interface.

User Perspective Guide - Complete Newsletter Workflow

Goal: Create, send, and track a newsletter campaign safely and effectively.

Audience: Non-technical users managing one-to-many email communications.

Quick Start

  • Prepare your audience: ensure subscribers have consent and valid emails.
  • Choose or create a template: pick a layout and set your brand basics.
  • Compose content: write a clear subject, concise body, and add images/links.
  • Test and approve: send test emails, check links, and get sign-off if needed.
  • Schedule or send: set a time window or send immediately.
  • Monitor results: watch delivery, opens, clicks, and unsubscribes.

Prerequisites

  • Verified sender identity: domain authenticated (SPF/DKIM) and from-address approved.
  • Consent and compliance: subscribers opted in; unsubscribe link present in footer.
  • Clean list: remove hard bounces and duplicates; honor suppression lists.

Create Your Audience

  • Add subscribers: import CSV or add manually with email and key attributes.
  • Segment: filter by tags or behaviors (e.g., "active in last 30 days").
  • Suppression: exclude unsubscribed, hard bounces, and prior complaints.

Compose Your Newsletter

  • Subject line: clear, benefit-led, under ~60 characters; avoid spammy words.
  • From name and reply-to: recognizable brand and monitored inbox.
  • Template: select a responsive layout; keep text-to-image balanced.
  • Links and tracking: use UTM parameters for analytics; validate all links.
  • Accessibility: add alt text to images; keep contrast and readable font sizes.

Preview & Test

  • Rendering: preview desktop/mobile; check dark mode if available.
  • Test sends: send to yourself and a small internal list; verify spam folder.
  • QA checklist: subject, from, reply-to, footer/legal, unsubscribe link, all links/images.
  • A/B testing (optional): try two subject lines or hero blocks; define success metric.

Schedule & Send

  • Timing: choose audience-friendly hours; respect time zones and quiet hours.
  • Throttling: use rate limits if warming up a domain or large sends.
  • Final review: confirm target segment size and exclusions; lock content version.
  • Launch: start the send or schedule; watch initial health indicators.

Monitor & Follow Up

  • Delivery: track sent, bounces (soft vs hard), and complaints; investigate spikes.
  • Engagement: open rate, CTR, unsubscribes; compare A/B variants.
  • Suppressions: auto-suppress complaints/hard bounces; update lists promptly.
  • Learnings: note top-performing links, sections, and subject patterns for next time.
  • Next action: send a reminder to non-openers (respect frequency caps) or follow-up content.

Compliance & Safety

  • Always include unsubscribe and physical address; avoid deceptive subjects.
  • Honor consent and preferences; provide clear opt-out.
  • Respect data privacy: do not expose PII in content or links.

Troubleshooting

  • High bounce rate: pause sends, validate list quality, check domain authentication.
  • Low opens: revisit subject line, send timing, and sender reputation.
  • Links broken: run link checker; fix redirects and tracking parameters.
  • Spam folder: reduce aggressive wording, improve text ratio, and warm up sending.
  • Complaints: tighten targeting, frequency caps, and expectation-setting at opt-in.

Best Practices

  • One primary message and one CTA; keep layout scannable.
  • Relevance beats volume: segment by interests and recency.
  • Maintain cadence: predictable schedule, but avoid over-sending.
  • Iterate: test small changes regularly and document what works.

  • Proactive communication: comment on tasks before issues become blockers.
  • Reasonable workload: don't overcommit; be realistic about capacity.
  • Complete tasks: finish tasks fully before marking complete; avoid partial completion.
  • Meet quotas: ensure daily newsletter generation and review requirements are met.
  • Timely reviews: approve pending items promptly to avoid bottlenecks.

Troubleshooting

Conclusion

The Task Management system is the central hub for all daily operations, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks. User Perspective Guide - Complete Data Management Workflow

Goal: Organize, validate, secure, and use your data consistently across modules.

Audience: Admins, data stewards, and power users managing records, schemas, and policies.

Quick Start

  • Create a dataset: define fields, types, and required validations.
  • Import data: upload CSV/XLSX or connect to an integration; preview mapping.
  • Clean data: run validation checks, deduplicate, and merge duplicates.
  • Tag sensitive fields: mark PII and set retention/visibility rules.
  • Set permissions: assign who can view, edit, export, or delete.
  • Monitor quality: track errors, completeness, and changes via the dashboard.

Prerequisites

  • Roles and permissions: admin/data-steward access for schema and governance.
  • Field standards: naming rules, data types, and accepted value lists.
  • Policies: retention windows, consent records, and deletion procedures.
  • Integrations: CRM/marketing/finance connectors and API keys configured.

Catalog & Schema

  • Datasets: create logical collections (e.g., Customers, Leads, Projects).
  • Fields: set type (text, number, date, enum, email, phone), required, default, unique.
  • Relationships: link datasets (e.g., Customer ‚Üí Proposals); enforce referential integrity.
  • Versioning: edit schema safely; keep migration history and rollback options.
  • Metadata: descriptions, owners, tags, and data lineage references.

Import & Capture

  • File upload: map columns to fields; set parsing rules and date formats.
  • Integrations: sync from external systems; choose full or incremental imports.
  • Forms: enable manual entry with inline validation and field help text.
  • Staging: preview imported rows; fix mapping issues before finalizing.
  • Schedules: automate recurring imports; alert on failures.

Data Quality

  • Validation rules: required fields, pattern checks, ranges, and enum memberships.
  • Deduplication: configure match keys (email/phone/name+company); merge strategy.
  • Standardization: normalize casing, trim spaces, format phone/country codes.
  • Completeness: track fill rates; prioritize fields critical to workflows.
  • Error handling: quarantine bad records; bulk fix with guided actions.

Governance

  • PII classification: tag sensitive fields (name, email, phone, address).
  • Consent and lawful basis: store opt-in source, timestamp, and scope of use.
  • Retention: auto-archive or delete after policy windows; log actions.
  • Right-to-be-forgotten: process deletion requests; propagate to linked datasets.
  • Audit trail: record creates/updates/deletes, user, timestamp, and changes.

Access & Security

  • Role-based access: dataset and field-level permissions (view/edit/export).
  • Sharing controls: restrict exports and API read access for sensitive data.
  • Approvals: require manager sign-off for bulk edits or large exports.
  • Masking: hide PII in UI lists; reveal on demand for authorized roles.
  • Alerts: notify owners on access violations or unusual activity.

Backup & Recovery

  • Snapshots: schedule backups; include schemas and records.
  • Restore: recover to a point-in-time; validate integrity post-restore.
  • Export: generate CSV/JSON for safe off-platform storage when permitted.
  • Continuity: document RPO/RTO and test recovery scenarios periodically.

Monitoring & Reporting

  • Dashboards: data quality score, error trends, completeness by field.
  • Change logs: daily/weekly summaries of adds, edits, deletes.
  • Usage: who accessed/exported which datasets; top queries.
  • Health checks: integration sync status, import success rate, latency.

Troubleshooting

  • Import fails: check column mapping, types, and date formats; inspect staging errors.
  • Duplicate records: refine match keys; run merge with reviewed conflict resolution.
  • Permission denied: verify role and dataset/field privileges; request access change.
  • Validation errors: adjust rules or correct data; use bulk fix tools.
  • Missing data: confirm integration filters, schedules, and API credentials.

Best Practices

  • Single source of truth: maintain canonical datasets; avoid shadow copies.
  • Small, safe changes: evolve schemas with versioning and migration notes.
  • Minimum PII: collect only what's necessary; mask and limit exports.
  • Automate checks: scheduled validations and dedupe runs reduce manual effort.
  • Document conventions: field definitions, accepted values, and business rules.
